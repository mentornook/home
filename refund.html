Refund and Cancellation Policy

Effective Date: 1 April 2025

At MENTOR NOOK, we strive to provide a fair and transparent refund and cancellation process for both students and teachers. This policy outlines the conditions under which refunds are issued and service charges are applied.

1. Refund Policy

If a class is disrupted due to a technical error or any other dispute, the refund will be calculated based on the time the class was successfully conducted. The fee distribution follows this structure:

  • The total fee paid by the student is divided based on the actual class duration.
  • The company deducts 15% from the tutor’s earnings as a platform service fee.
  • The company deducts 5% from the student’s refund as a service charge.

Example Calculation:

  • Total Fee Paid by Student: 100 bucks
  • Class Duration: 1 hour (60 minutes)
  • Actual Class Time Held: 40 minutes
  • Time Ratio: 40/60 = 2/3
  • Proportional Tutor Earnings: (2/3) × 100 = 67 bucks
  • Company’s 15% Fee from Tutor: 67 × 15% = 10.05 bucks
  • Company’s 5% Fee from Student: (100 - 67) × 5% = 1.65 bucks

Final Distribution:

  • Tutor Receives: 67 - 10.05 = 56.95 bucks
  • Student Refund: (100 - 67) - 1.65 = 31.35 bucks
  • Company Earns: 10.05 (Tutor Fee) + 1.65 (Student Service Charge) = 11.7 bucks

2. Cancellation Policy

  • If a class is canceled before it starts, the student will receive a refund after deducting a 10% service charge.
  • If a class is canceled mid-session, the refund will be calculated based on the time ratio as described above.
  • If a teacher cancels a class, students receive a full refund minus the 10% service charge as a penalty to the tutor.
  • If a student cancels a class, students receive a full refund minus the 10% service charge.

3. Non-Refundable Situations

  • If a student or teacher violates platform policies (e.g., abusive language, prohibited content, rule violations), they will not be eligible for a refund.
  • If a student fails to attend the class without prior notice, no refund is issued.
  • If a tutor fails to conduct a class without valid reasoning, they may face penalties.
